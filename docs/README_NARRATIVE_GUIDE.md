# README Narrative Guide

## Purpose

This repository should present Startup Graveyard as an open-source failure intelligence platform, not as a novelty archive of failed startups.

The README is the primary GitHub landing page. It must help a first-time visitor understand, within a few seconds:

1. What the product is
2. Why the product matters
3. What stage the product is in

## Core positioning

Use this sentence as the north-star description:

> Startup Graveyard is an open-source failure intelligence platform that turns startup postmortems into structured, queryable, explainable research assets.

## What to emphasize

- Research workflows over story consumption
- Structured case intelligence over anecdotal browsing
- Runnable alpha product over concept-only repository
- Open-source credibility over inflated marketing language
- Reusable outputs such as saved views, briefs, exports, and team workflows

## What to de-emphasize

These areas are real, but they should not dominate the top of the README:

- Billing portal details
- Recovery queue internals
- Dead-letter and outreach automation details
- Admin workflow implementation details
- Low-level ops instrumentation

Keep those deeper details in later sections or supporting docs.

## Tone rules

- English-first, with optional Chinese support lines
- Professional and precise
- Research-product framing
- No inflated scale claims
- No enterprise theater

Avoid phrases like:

- industry-leading
- enterprise-ready
- production-grade at scale
- hundreds of cases

Prefer phrases like:

- runnable alpha
- structured failure research
- grounded analysis
- reusable research assets
- open-source failure intelligence

## Fact guardrails

You may say:

- the product is runnable
- the product is alpha
- the repository includes public, admin, and platform capabilities
- the seed dataset includes 40+ published cases

You should not imply:

- broad production adoption
- large-scale enterprise maturity
- significantly larger dataset coverage than currently exists

## README section order

Use this order unless there is a strong reason to change it:

1. Hero
2. Why this exists
3. What Startup Graveyard is
4. Who it is for
5. What you can do with it
6. How it works
7. Product architecture
8. Why it is different
9. Current status
10. Quickstart
11. Local URLs
12. Tech stack
13. Testing strategy
14. Product maturity roadmap
15. Repository highlights
16. Contributing
17. Limitations

## Maintenance rule

If product capability expands faster than the public narrative, update the public narrative to stay aligned. Do not let README, homepage, and metadata drift into different versions of the product story.
